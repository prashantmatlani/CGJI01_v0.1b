Archetypes are universal patterns or structures within the collective unconscious. They manifest as recurring symbols, themes, and characters across cultures and time.

Common archetypes include the Hero, the Mother, the Trickster, and the Wise Old Man. These figures appear in myths, dreams, and literature.

Archetypes are not fixed images but dynamic tendencies that shape human experience and perception.

They influence behavior, emotions, and imagination, often operating outside conscious awareness.

Understanding archetypes allows individuals to recognize deeper patterns in their lives and connect personal experiences to universal themes.
