The shadow represents the unconscious aspects of the personality that the ego does not recognize or accept. It includes traits, desires, and impulses that are often repressed or denied.

The shadow is not purely negative; it also contains undeveloped potential, creativity, and vitality. However, when unacknowledged, it can manifest in destructive or irrational behavior.

Shadow projection occurs when individuals attribute their own unconscious traits to others. This can lead to conflict, misunderstanding, and moral judgment.

Confronting the shadow is a crucial step in individuation. It requires honesty, self-reflection, and the willingness to face uncomfortable truths about oneself.

Integrating the shadow leads to greater authenticity, emotional maturity, and psychological balance.
