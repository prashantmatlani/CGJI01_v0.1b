
The anima is a central concept in Jungian analytical psychology. It refers to the unconscious feminine aspect within the psyche of a man. Its counterpart in women is the animus.

Jung described the anima as a mediator between the conscious ego and the unconscious. It often appears in dreams, fantasies, and projections as a female figure, symbolizing emotional depth, intuition, and relational sensitivity.

The anima develops through stages, ranging from primitive biological representations to more refined spiritual and symbolic forms. These stages reflect the individual's psychological development.

An unresolved or unconscious anima may lead to projections onto external figures, particularly in romantic relationships. This can result in idealization or emotional dependency.

The integration of the anima is essential for individuation. By recognizing and integrating this inner figure, an individual gains access to deeper emotional awareness and psychological balance.
