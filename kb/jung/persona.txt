The persona is the social mask or role that an individual presents to the world. It is shaped by cultural expectations, social norms, and personal adaptation.

While the persona is necessary for functioning in society, over-identification with it can lead to a loss of authenticity. The individual may become disconnected from their inner life.

A rigid persona can prevent the expression of genuine emotions and desires. It may also contribute to internal conflict when the true self diverges from the social role.

The process of individuation involves loosening the grip of the persona and reconnecting with the deeper layers of the psyche.

A healthy persona remains flexible and serves as a bridge between the individual and society, rather than a barrier.
