Individuation is the central process in Jungian psychology, referring to the development of the individual toward psychological wholeness.

It involves the integration of unconscious contents into conscious awareness, including the shadow, anima or animus, and other archetypal elements.

The goal of individuation is not perfection but completeness. It requires confronting inner conflicts and reconciling opposites within the psyche.

This process often unfolds through dreams, symbols, and life experiences that challenge the ego’s assumptions.

Individuation leads to the emergence of the Self, which represents the totality of the psyche and a deeper sense of identity beyond the ego.
