The collective unconscious is a foundational concept in Jungian psychology. It refers to a layer of the unconscious mind shared by all humans.

Unlike the personal unconscious, which is shaped by individual experience, the collective unconscious contains inherited patterns and archetypes.

These archetypes structure human perception and behavior, influencing dreams, myths, and cultural expressions.

The collective unconscious connects individuals to a broader human experience, transcending personal history.

Accessing this layer of the psyche can provide profound insight, but it also requires careful interpretation to avoid projection and misunderstanding.
